Processor (CPU)

The CPU is the "brain" of your PC, and it performs most of the calculations that are required to run your software. When choosing a CPU, you'll need to consider factors like clock speed, core count, and power consumption. Popular CPU manufacturers include Intel and AMD.

Motherboard

The motherboard is the main circuit board in your PC, and it connects all of the components together. When choosing a motherboard, you'll need to consider factors like socket type (to match your CPU), chipset, and form factor. Popular motherboard manufacturers include Asus, Gigabyte, and MSI.

Memory (RAM)

RAM (Random Access Memory) is used by your PC to store data that it needs to access quickly. When choosing RAM, you'll need to consider factors like capacity (measured in gigabytes), speed, and latency. Popular RAM manufacturers include Corsair, G.Skill, and Kingston.

Storage (HDD or SSD)

Storage is used to store your operating system, software, and files. There are two main types of storage: HDD (Hard Disk Drive) and SSD (Solid State Drive). SSDs are faster and more expensive, while HDDs are slower and less expensive. You'll need to consider factors like capacity and speed when choosing storage. Popular storage manufacturers include Seagate, Western Digital, and Samsung.

Graphics Card (GPU)

The GPU is responsible for rendering graphics and video in your PC. When choosing a GPU, you'll need to consider factors like performance, power consumption, and compatibility with your other components. Popular GPU manufacturers include Nvidia and AMD.

Power Supply (PSU)

The power supply converts the electricity from your wall outlet into a form that your PC can use. When choosing a PSU, you'll need to consider factors like wattage, efficiency, and modular vs non-modular design. Popular PSU manufacturers include Corsair, EVGA, and Seasonic.

Casing

The casing is the outer shell of your PC, and it protects your components from dust and other debris. When choosing a casing, you'll need to consider factors like size, form factor, and aesthetics. Popular casing manufacturers include Fractal Design, NZXT, and Phanteks.
